Macramé Basics: Your First Wall Hanging

Macramé Beginner

What you'll need

  • BIG CORD 5mm Cotton Core – 30 meters (natural or your favourite colour)
  • Wooden dowel or driftwood – approx. 40 cm
  • Scissors
  • Measuring tape
  • Optional: wooden beads for decoration

Step 1: Cut your cords

Start by cutting 12 pieces of cord, each approximately 2.5 meters long. These will form the body of your wall hanging. Don't worry about being too precise – macramé is forgiving!

Step 2: Attach cords to the dowel

Fold each cord in half and attach it to the dowel using a Lark's Head Knot. Place the folded loop behind the dowel, then pull the two loose ends through the loop. Pull tight. Repeat for all 12 cords.

Step 3: Create Square Knots

Take four adjacent cords. The two outer cords are your working cords, and the two inner cords are your filler cords. Create a square knot by:

  1. Cross the left cord over the two filler cords and under the right cord.
  2. Take the right cord under the filler cords and up through the loop on the left.
  3. Pull tight. This is the first half of the knot.
  4. Now mirror the process: right over fillers, left under and up.
  5. Pull tight. You've made one complete square knot!

Step 4: Alternate the pattern

For the next row, skip the first two cords and take the next four to make a square knot. Continue across the row. This creates the classic alternating pattern. Repeat for 8-10 rows or until you reach your desired length.

Finishing touches

Once you've knotted enough rows, trim the bottom ends to create a clean or angled fringe. You can brush out the cord ends with a fine-tooth comb for a fluffy, boho look. Attach a piece of cord to both ends of the dowel for hanging.

Tip: Try mixing two colours of cord for a striking two-tone effect! Check out our shop for the full range of colours.
